TERMS OF START-UP WORLD

Accelerator (aka Incubator):

Not part of a car as one might assume, but a center where start-ups are ‘incubated’ through mentorship, space and sometimes cash. Some examples of accelerators are Y Combinator, 500 Startups and Techstars.

B-to-B = Business to Business:

A company sells its products to other companies. It can be supplying components or directly selling the end product. An example - Samsung has signed a deal with Apple to supply OLED panels that will be used in iPhones.

B-to-C = Business to Consumer:

A company sells its products to the masses. Any product you buy from the market is an example.

Bootstrapping:

It means starting a business without external help or capital. Such start-ups fund the development of their company through internal cash flow and are cautious with their expenses. For example, two college dropouts start a venture using their own money.

What Non-Millennials Can’t Tell You about Modern Entrepreneurship

Going back about 30 years ago, entrepreneurship either meant owning a multi-million dollar company, or being a failure. Today on the other hand is a very different scenario. With today’s ever increasing needs and skills, the word "entrepreneur" holds more meaning than ever before. Today, all one needs is ambition, fuelled by passion to be an entrepreneur. Success, today, is subjective. Today, we have more options than ever before.

But then comes the question of how one can become an entrepreneur. This question is something that everyone needs answers to, and the answers are never set in stone. Non-Millennials do not know how modern entrepreneurship works, so looking for experienced opinion is a tad tough. That is why I will try to answer that question based on the knowledge I’ve gathered from all the entrepreneurs I have studied:

  • One does not need capital to be successful: The thing about modern entrepreneurship is that passion, ambition and determination is the most important investment one needs to make. They create the foundations of a company, not capital. You may lose your team, you may not see growth in your idea for quite some time; but if you are passionate and determined, then your ambition will come to fruition.
  • One almost never has anything to lose: A very important aspect of being an entrepreneur is to understand that if you believe in your idea, and you give it a 100%, then all you have to lose is time and effort you put into it. However, everyone knows that time and effort pay back in full - if not more - eventually. If you are ever afraid of what might happen when taking a risk, remember: people are friendly and they will support you if your idea appeals to them.
  • Things don’t fall in place; you make them fall in place: An entrepreneur must never sit back. People will almost never come to you - you must go to them. You can’t expect the world to know about your idea if you don’t put your idea out in the world. You can’t expect your idea to gain reputation if you don’t force your idea to. One must remember that as one grows, so does their company, and vice-versa. So, one must push oneself constantly to reach new heights.
  • Storytelling and branding: Your idea must appeal to the public. Whatever target audience you choose your product for must feel the need for your product. To make them feel the need for your product, you need to give them a story. Framing your story for any product is eventually what your idea will stand for, and that is what makes your idea special – branded. If you wish to be unique, turn your company into a brand.
  • Don’t overdo: There is something called “Imposter Syndrome” wherein you may think you aren’t as good as some other companies with similar ideas. Irrespective of whether that is the case, this thought will cause problems for you. Do not overwork at any point in time. Find a balance of satisfaction and progress, and that will help you to keep going at it.


I hope I have answered the questions any entrepreneur would have regarding how to begin and stay with your idea. I would like to conclude my answer with one final note: if you ever find yourself in a problem you don’t have a solution to, remember to ask for help. Help is always available.
